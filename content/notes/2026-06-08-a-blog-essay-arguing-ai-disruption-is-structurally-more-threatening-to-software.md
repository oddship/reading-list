**What it is:** a blog essay arguing AI disruption is structurally more threatening to software than many other fields because code is verifiable, open source created a huge training corpus, and AI labs can dogfood coding tools on themselves.

**Gist:** the author expects a race to the bottom in software pricing, wage compression, permanent erosion of the talent pipeline, higher output expectations for remaining engineers, and most upside captured by owners/model providers.

**Newsletter angle:** software may be the cleanest early target for AI because it has both an oracle and the richest public corpus.
