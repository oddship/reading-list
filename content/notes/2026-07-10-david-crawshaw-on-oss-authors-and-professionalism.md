**What it is:** X post by David Crawshaw commenting on expectations of professionalism from OSS authors

**Gist:** Argues that independent open-source authors do not have to behave like salaried representatives of institutions. His point is that people used to company-backed open source often expect a professionalized posture by default, but truly independent authors are shipping work on their own terms and do not owe that style of conduct.

**Newsletter angle:** Useful side-angle on the Andrew/Jarred/Bun argument because it reframes part of the conflict as a mismatch between startup or company expectations and the norms of independent open-source authorship.
